Biography

Hayao Miyazaki is a world-renowned Japanese animator, director, and co-founder of Studio Ghibli. He was born on January 5, 1941, in Tokyo, Japan. Miyazaki began his career as an animator in the 1960s, working at various studios before co-founding Studio Ghibli in 1985 with fellow director Isao Takahata.

Miyazaki is best known for his imaginative storytelling, deep environmental themes, and strong female characters. His films, such as My Neighbor Totoro, Princess Mononoke, and Spirited Away, have received international acclaim and have become beloved by audiences around the world.

In 2003, Spirited Away won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature, making Miyazaki the first Japanese director to receive this honor. Even after announcing retirement several times, he continues to return to filmmaking, driven by his passion and creativity. His latest film, The Boy and the Heron (2023), marks another milestone in his legendary career.
